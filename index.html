What We Do

We offer a variety of activities year-round. No experience is necessary to participate.

  • Dissections

    We perform several dissections every year, including sheep brains and cow eyes. This is a great hands-on opportunity to learn about neuroanatomy and have fun!

  • EEG

    EEG stands for electroencephalogram. It's a device that measures brain waves! We do demonstrations where you can try it on and see your own brain waves, and we also develop applications for its use.

  • Brain Bee

    The Brain Bee is the largest neuroscience competition for high school students. Our members attend the CSU East Bay "Bay Bee" and the Stanford Brain Bee. The 1st place winner goes to nationals in Baltimore, MD. For the past two years, we have won 1st at regionals!

  • Guest Speakers

    Guest speakers from local universities are invited on a regular basis to give talks about their work in different aspects of neuroscience. For example, two Stanford students came in April to discuss their work on the visual cortex.

  • Lab Tours

    We visit labs in which ongoing experiments are occurring and get the chance to network with neuroscience professionals. These tours are open to all of our members.

  • Interactive Learning

    Want to see Pranav wearing an afro AND learn about neuroscience simultaneously? Our weekly meetings are the only place where you can find both of these great activities!
